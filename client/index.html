Our Instructors

Robert Schwentker

DLT Education President, and Founding President of Blockchain University. He has 25+ years in technical education services. Previously he led developer and startup programs at PayPal and American Express. Robert holds 30+ IBM Certifications as Developer, Administrator & Instructor.

Ryan X. Charles

A bitcoin engineer who has implemented the bitcoin protocol and many bitcoin standards in javascript, including payment protocol and stealth addresses. Previously he was at BitGo where he worked on the multisig bitcoin security platform, and also at BitPay where he authored the Bitcore library.

Jonathan Levi

Co-Founder of a Blockchain Startup, and Core Developer of the Hyperledger Fabric. His background includes leading teams at Goldman Sachs and Barclays Capital and was a Senior Software Engineer at Cisco Systems.

Steven Zeiler

Software architect who helped build and evangelize Ripple and Interledger. He’s contriubuted to and built numerous DLT projects including blockchain account monitors, p2p network daemons, validator registries, vault clients, smart oracle hosts & command line interfaces.

Dr. Jeff Flowers

Senior Blockchain Instructor. He led Curriculum & Instructional Design at Blockchain University. Currently he is Professor of Chemistry at College of San Mateo. Jeff holds an Ed.D in Educational Leadership from San Francisco State University.

Makoto Takemiya

Co-Founder and CEO of Soramitsu Company based in Tokyo, Japan. Soramitsu has open sourced Iroha which is now part of Hyperledger. He was born in California and is now a Japanese citizen. Makoto is one of Iroha’s core developers, and travels globally to evangelize Hyperledger and Iroha.

Alexandra Groetsema

Cofounder & lead developer at RiskBazaar. Web developer, entrepreneur & teacher assistant. Additionally she attended Blockchain University as a Women in Tech Diversity scholar. Alexandra received her BA from UC Berkeley.

João Paulo

Chief Blockchain Officer at Foxbit, one of Brazil’s top cryptocurrency exchanges. He’s built out numerous blockchain related APIs & SDKs and building out smart contracts and oracles in Ethereum.

Anish Mohammed

Security and cryptography researcher and consultant for the past 15 years. He has spent half his career researching cryptographic algorithms and protocols. He is on the advisory board for Adjoint, Arteia, Privacy Shell, Ripple Labs and Chain of Things. Currently focused on projects that involve security vs scalability vs consensus of Blockchain and using smart contracts for AI safety.
